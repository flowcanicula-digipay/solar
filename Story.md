## Who Is SolarTNP?

SolarTNP is a newly established **Vietnamese** solar energy company, with
global sourcing partnerships and a deep, defining tie to Japan. It sells,
designs, installs, and maintains residential and light-commercial solar panel
systems — and separately, sources solar equipment and ships it to clients
abroad. The company is based in Phú Nhuận, Ho Chi Minh City — and serves
homeowners, small businesses, and light-industrial clients across southern
Vietnam, plus international buyers in the US and Southeast Asia.

### The Identity — Why It Matters

This is not a marketing angle. It is the operational reality of how the
company works, and it directly explains why SolarTNP operates the way it does.

**Vietnamese identity — the crew, the ownership, the client relationship.**
The installation crew, the project managers, the ownership, and the
client-facing team are Vietnamese. They know Vietnamese roof construction,
Vietnamese EVN bureaucracy, Vietnamese monsoon seasons, and Vietnamese client
concerns — especially the one about roof waterproofing that foreign installers
consistently underestimate. Vietnamese culture values long-term relationship
over transactional speed. A SolarTNP client isn't a sale closed — they're a
household or business the team will be checking on for years.

**Japanese standards — the company's core technical identity.**
Japan has some of the most demanding electrical and construction standards in
the world, and Japanese consumers are notoriously unforgiving of quality
failures. This is not one heritage among equals — Japanese electrical
standards and a 25-year mindset govern every install: mounting hardware
torqued to spec, cable runs planned before they're laid, every connection
tested before the inverter is switched on. A solar system is a 25-year
commitment, and the installation decisions made on day one determine
performance for decades. SolarTNP installs as if they'll be back to service it
every year. (They will be.)

**Global partners — sourcing leverage without picking one country.**
Deep supplier relationships span the global solar manufacturing chain — not
centered on any single country. This means SolarTNP can evaluate panel
quality at the manufacturing level, not just the spec sheet, and source
components (including Japanese- and Chinese-made panels and inverters) at
competitive prices without compromising on quality.

**International sourcing & shipping — a distinct service line.**
Beyond sourcing for its own Vietnamese installs, SolarTNP also sources solar
equipment and ships it to clients abroad, across the **US and Southeast
Asia**, drawing on the same supplier network. This is not folded silently
into "global partners" — it's a separate offer with its own page (`/sourcing`)
and its own inquiry form, because the buyer (a business abroad placing an
equipment order) has nothing in common with a Vietnamese homeowner requesting
a site assessment.

**What the combination produces**: a company that sources as well as any
international distributor, installs to Japanese electrical standards, and
navigates the Vietnamese permitting, grid-connection, and after-sales
landscape as natives. No other solar installer in Biên Hòa or southern Vietnam
brings all of this together.

**SolarTNP is brand new.** It has completed only a handful of installations —
4 at launch. The site must feel credible and professional without overstating
experience. The trust is built through specificity: real system sizes, real
panel brands, real project locations, real warranty terms, and the honesty to
say "we are just starting and we do fewer jobs and we do them properly."

**Tagline**: *"Trusted sourcing. Japanese standards. Vietnamese craft."*

## The Complete Service Journey — 8 Phases

This is the full process SolarTNP takes every client through, from first contact
to long-term relationship. Every page of the site reinforces stages of this
journey. Copy, headings, and schema must reflect this accurately.

### Phase 1 — Lead & Consultation (Free)
A prospective client contacts SolarTNP via the website form, Zalo, phone, or
referral. The first conversation is a qualifying discussion — not a pitch.
Key questions: What is your average monthly electricity bill? What type of
property? Do you own the roof? Are you on a residential or commercial EVN
tariff? The answers determine whether solar makes financial sense for this
client right now, and roughly what system size is appropriate.

**Tone for this phase**: No pressure. If solar doesn't make sense for someone's
situation, SolarTNP says so. This honesty is a brand differentiator.

### Phase 2 — Free Site Assessment
A SolarTNP technician visits the property. They:
- Measure usable roof area and note orientation/tilt angle
- Identify shading sources (trees, water tanks, neighbouring buildings)
- Assess roof structure, condition, and material (tile, metal, concrete flat)
- Inspect the existing electrical distribution board
- Confirm the EVN connection type (single-phase or three-phase)
- Verify building legal compliance (required under Decree 135/2024/ND-CP)

This visit is completely free and non-binding. The client owes nothing.

### Phase 3 — System Design & Written Proposal
Back at the office, the team (drawing on global sourcing knowledge and
Japanese engineering standards) designs the system:
- Panel count, layout, and brand/model selection
- Inverter sizing and brand/model selection
- Cable routing plan
- Mounting method specific to that roof type
- Estimated monthly generation (kWh/month by season)
- Estimated monthly bill saving (VND)
- Projected payback period (years)

The client receives a **written proposal** covering: full bill of materials
(panel brand/model, inverter brand/model, mounting hardware specs), total
installed price (one number — no hidden extras), estimated annual generation,
warranty summary per component, and the EVN approval timeline for their
specific province.

### Phase 4 — Contract & DOIT Notification
Once the client accepts the proposal, they sign an installation contract.
SolarTNP immediately files the simplified notification with the provincial
Department of Industry and Trade (DOIT) for systems under 100 kW — this runs
in parallel, not after, so no time is wasted.

### Phase 5 — Procurement & Component Inspection
SolarTNP orders the specific panels and inverter for this job. Components arrive
at the Phú Nhuận facility, are inspected on receipt against the manufacturer
spec sheet, and staged for the installation date. The global supplier
relationships mean components arrive quickly and at verified quality.

### Phase 6 — Installation (2–4 days depending on system size)
The SolarTNP crew — the same team, no subcontractors — arrives on the agreed
date. Typical schedule:
- **Day 1**: Roof mounting hardware installation, cable trunking and conduit
- **Day 2**: Panel placement, DC wiring, waterproofing of all roof penetrations
- **Day 3** (if needed): Inverter installation, AC connection to distribution
  board, system commissioning and testing
- **Final day**: Monitoring app configuration and handover walkthrough with the
  client. The site is fully cleaned before the crew leaves.

Japanese electrical standards govern every connection and every cable run.
Waterproofing of all roof penetrations is treated as a first-class concern —
SolarTNP knows Vietnamese homeowners' primary fear is a leaking roof.

### Phase 7 — EVN Grid Connection & Metering
SolarTNP submits all post-installation documentation to EVN and schedules the
grid inspection. An EVN engineer visits, inspects the installation, and if
satisfied, installs bi-directional metering — so surplus electricity up to the
20% cap (Decree 135/2024/ND-CP) can be tracked and credited. SolarTNP manages
this entire process. Typical timeline: 2–6 weeks from submission to
energisation, depending on the province and EVN office workload.

### Phase 8 — Formal Handover
Once EVN has connected and the system is live, a formal handover meeting at the
property. The client receives a physical handover package containing:
- Panel manufacturer warranty documents (25-year linear output guarantee)
- Inverter manufacturer warranty documents
- SolarTNP workmanship warranty certificate (2 years)
- SolarTNP mounting hardware warranty certificate (10 years)
- SolarTNP roof waterproofing warranty certificate (2 years)
- EVN metering confirmation letter
- DOIT notification receipt
- System schematic (as-built drawing)
- Monitoring app credentials and user guide

The technician walks through the first month's expected generation numbers so
the client knows what normal looks like.

## After-Sales & Customer Support

This section is as important as the installation itself for the site narrative.
Solar systems run 25+ years. SolarTNP's after-sales offering is what separates
them from operators who disappear after the final payment.

### 12-Month Free Support
For the first 12 months after handover, **all callouts are free**. Any issue —
a panel underperforming, an inverter error code, a cable connection needing
adjustment — SolarTNP responds at no charge. This is not a promotion; it is
the baseline. Copy should present this as standard expectation, not a gift.

### Remote Monitoring
The monitoring app (connected to the inverter) gives the client real-time
generation data, historical production graphs, and fault alerts. SolarTNP also
has visibility into all client systems on its monitoring dashboard. If a system
drops below expected output without an obvious weather explanation, SolarTNP
contacts the client proactively — before the client notices something is wrong.
This proactive monitoring is a meaningful differentiator; most installers only
respond when the client calls.

### Annual Maintenance Plan (from Year 2)
An optional subscription service covering:
- **Two panel cleaning visits per year** — dust and bird deposits cost 5–15%
  output in Vietnam's dry season; cleaning recovers it fully
- **Annual technical inspection** — torque check on all mounting fasteners,
  DC and AC connection integrity test, inverter health check, performance
  review against original design estimates
- **Priority response** — maintenance plan clients are scheduled before
  non-plan clients for any service callout

### Warranty Structure

Every SolarTNP installation carries a layered warranty — manufacturer warranties
backed by SolarTNP's own workmanship guarantee. Copy must present this as a
table or structured list, not buried in prose.

| Component | Warranty provider | Duration | Coverage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Solar panels | Manufacturer (Panasonic / Sharp / Kyocera) | 25 years | Linear output guarantee — ≤0.45%/yr degradation; at year 25, ≥89% of rated output |
| Inverter | Manufacturer (model-dependent) | 5–10 years | Parts and labour for manufacturing defects |
| Mounting hardware | SolarTNP | 10 years | Structural integrity — no failure due to corrosion or material defect |
| Workmanship | SolarTNP | 2 years | Any defect caused by installation — wiring, connections, assembly |
| Roof waterproofing | SolarTNP | 2 years | Any water ingress caused by the installation — penetrations, flashings, fixings |

**The roof waterproofing warranty is the one to lead with** in Vietnamese
context. It is the primary anxiety of Vietnamese homeowners considering a roof
installation, and most installers either don't offer it or bury it. SolarTNP
putting it in the table, explicitly, for 2 years, answers the anxiety directly.

### Warranty Claims Process
1. Client contacts SolarTNP by phone, Zalo, or email
2. SolarTNP performs remote diagnostic via the monitoring dashboard
3. If a site visit is needed: within 48 hours for active faults, within 5
   business days for non-urgent issues
4. For component defects under manufacturer warranty: SolarTNP manages the
   claim with the manufacturer — the client never has to deal with a Japanese
   or Chinese supplier directly
5. Repair or replacement confirmed in writing with the client

### Long-Term Relationship (Year 20+)
When panels approach end of useful life, SolarTNP proactively contacts long-term
clients to discuss panel refresh or system upgrade options. This positions the
company as a 25-year energy partner, not a one-time installer. Copy should
reference this briefly in the After-Sales section — it signals seriousness
and long-term thinking without overpromising.

### Regulations (`/regulations`)
A dedicated page explaining Vietnam's Decree 135/2024/ND-CP and what it means
for SolarTNP's clients. This is both an SEO asset (specific, authoritative
content that AI crawlers can cite) and a trust signal (we know the law, we'll
handle the paperwork). Key sections:

**Page hero** (subdued — same nav/footer, just a section heading):
- Title: "Vietnam Solar Regulations — What You Need to Know"
- Subtitle: "Decree 135/2024/ND-CP governs rooftop solar in Vietnam. We've
  summarised what it means for your home or business installation — and how
  SolarTNP handles compliance for you."

**Section 1 — The Core Rule: Self-Consumption First**
Under Decree 135/2024/ND-CP, rooftop solar systems in Vietnam must be
installed primarily for on-site, self-consumption. Your system capacity must
generally match your existing electricity load at the time of registration.
This is a feature, not a limitation — it means the system is sized correctly
for your actual needs, not oversold.

**Section 2 — Selling Surplus Back to EVN (the 20% cap)**
You are permitted to export excess electricity to Vietnam Electricity (EVN),
but this is strictly capped at 20% of your installed capacity. Surplus power
sold to EVN is compensated at the preceding year's average electricity market
price — not the older, higher feed-in tariffs. The honest implication: the
financial case for solar is now built primarily on self-consumption savings,
not surplus export revenue.

**Section 3 — Off-Grid Exception**
If your system is completely disconnected from the national grid, there are no
capacity limits. This applies to remote properties or businesses choosing full
energy independence with battery storage.

**Section 4 — Capacity Thresholds & Licensing**
Presented as a clean comparison table:

| System size | Who it covers | Process |
|---|---|---|
| Under 100 kW | Households, small businesses | Simplified notification to provincial DOIT — no investment approval needed |
| 100 kW – 1,000 kW | Mid-sized commercial/industrial | Standard permitting with local authorities |
| Over 1,000 kW (1 MW+) | Large commercial/industrial selling to grid | Development registration certificate + Power Operation License required |

**Section 5 — Building Compliance Requirements**
The rooftop system must be installed on buildings that fully comply with
construction law, land-use rights, fire safety standards, and environmental
regulations. SolarTNP verifies building compliance during the pre-installation
site assessment — we will not proceed on a building with unresolved compliance
issues.

**Section 6 — How SolarTNP Handles This**
- We size your system to your actual load — compliant by design
- We prepare and submit your DOIT notification (under 100 kW)
- We coordinate EVN grid connection approval and inspection
- We document the 20% surplus cap in your system design specs
- We do not install on non-compliant buildings

Regulations change — consult a licensed energy lawyer for binding advice.
Sources: SP Global, WFW, Vietnam Business Law, Vietnam Law Magazine,

## Tone & Voice

**Honest and grounded, never inflated.** SolarTNP is new. It has 4 completed
installations. The copy never pretends otherwise. Trust is earned through
specificity — real system sizes, real panel brand names, real warranty durations,
real province locations. Vagueness is the enemy of credibility for a new company.

**Technically confident, not jargon-heavy.** The founding team understands solar
at a supply-chain and engineering level that most Vietnamese installers don't.
The copy demonstrates this without alienating a homeowner who just wants to know
"will this save me money?" Lead with the concrete benefit, follow with the spec.

**One identity, one voice.** The Vietnamese-craft-plus-Japanese-standards
identity is not two separate personalities — it is a single, integrated
voice that is:
- Precise (global sourcing discipline — no vague estimates, no inflated claims)
- Exacting (Japanese engineering standard — "we torque every bolt to spec" is
  a real sentence, not a metaphor)
- Warm (Vietnamese relationship culture — clients are long-term partners, not
  transactions; the roof waterproofing warranty exists because SolarTNP knows
  what Vietnamese homeowners actually worry about)

**English**: Clear, internationally accessible, professional. Primary language
for the site. Foreign investors, expats, and international sourcing clients
will read this first.

**Vietnamese**: Natural, everyday phrasing. A Biên Hòa homeowner, a Thủ Đức
business owner. Key terms used naturally: "điện mặt trời", "hòa lưới điện",

**Japanese**: Polite-form (です/ます). Katakana for loanwords naturally

in Vietnam or considering Vietnam properties will be familiar with panel brands
(パナソニック, シャープ, 京セラ) — speak to them as insiders. The 25-year warranty
mindset resonates strongly with Japanese quality expectations — lean into it.
